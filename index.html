My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and planetary-scale sensing — training foundation models that map Earth's mineral resources via satellite hyperspectral imagery, and building physics-guided systems that extract geochemical signal from high-dimensional observational data to advance carbon capture and critical mineral discovery.

Research Pillars

Pillar I

Earth Observation & Soil Intelligence

Earth Intelligence Lab — Advised by Sherrie Wang & Evan Coleman

Physics-guided deep learning on NASA EMIT hyperspectral satellite imagery to infer soil geochemical properties at global scale. Core methods include spatiotemporal autoencoders that learn joint spectral-spatial embeddings, multimodal fusion of orbital, atmospheric, and field-collected measurements from YardStick PBC, and physics-constrained spectral reconstruction for interpretable, out-of-distribution-robust carbon inference.

Hyperspectral Remote Sensing Physics-Guided ML Soil Carbon NASA EMIT Spatiotemporal Autoencoders
Pillar II

Geospatial Foundation Models & Mineral Intelligence

MIT MCSC — Advised by Evan Coleman, Elsa Olivetti & Sherrie Wang

Continent-scale mineral prospecting through masked geospatial infilling over structured multi-layer grids encoding fault lines, elevation, rock type, and agronomic surveys. Adapts masked modeling pretraining strategies to geospatial segmentation, with direct implications for critical mineral supply chains and carbon capture feedstock discovery.

Geospatial AI Masked Modeling U-Net / ResNet Mineral Prospecting AAAI 2026
Pillar III

Computer Vision & Embodied Perception

Fifth Set Analytics  &  GT Robot Learning Lab — Advised by Danfei Xu

Applied computer vision for real-world perception tasks: automated sports officiating via fine-tuned pose estimation models (MMPose, MMDetection), and human hand-tracking for robotic manipulation using Intel RealSense D435 depth cameras in the context of Franka arm teleoperation. Demonstrates transfer of foundation model priors to constrained deployment environments.

Pose Estimation MMPose / MMDetection Robot Learning Intel RealSense
Pillar IV — Early Work

Astronomical Machine Learning

NASA JPL — Advised by Kyle Pearson  &  NASA Exoplanet Watch — Zellem, Tock

Early-career research on deep learning for exoplanet transit detection using phase-folded photometric light curves. Developed CNN+LSTM architectures trained on synthetic TESS-mimicking data and contributed to the EXOTIC citizen science pipeline, resulting in 10 publications across AAS, PASP, RNAAS, and SAS.

Exoplanet Detection CNN / LSTM Light Curve Analysis Citizen Science

Earth2EMIT: Spatiotemporal Autoencoders for Orbital-Proximal Fusion
Sujay Nair, Evan Coleman, Sherrie Wang
Nature Planned In Preparation

All-in-one soil property prediction pipeline fusing NASA EMIT hyperspectral satellite data with geospatial, atmospheric, and high-quality field measurements from YardStick PBC. Uses a physics-guided autoencoder that learns spectral-spatial embeddings to reconstruct soil spectra and predict soil properties.

[13]
Masked Mineral Modeling: Continent-Scale Mineral Prospecting via Geospatial Infilling
Sujay Nair, Evan Coleman, Elsa Olivetti, Sherrie Wang
AAAI 2026

Trained a U-Net ResNet to infill the presence of masked minerals based on surrounding mineral resources, geophysical data (fault lines, elevation, rock type), and agronomic data (RaCA). Model outperformed ViT and Kriging baselines with a test Dice coefficient of 0.31.

Paper
[12]
Structured Spectral Reconstruction for Scalable Soil Organic Carbon Inference
Evan Coleman, Sujay Nair, Xinyi Zeng, Elsa Olivetti
ICLR — Tackling Climate Change with ML Workshop 2024

Used USDA RaCA data to reconstruct soil organic carbon (SOC) spectra alongside SOC regression to improve out-of-distribution generalization. A physics-based reconstruction pipeline provides an interpretable spectral signature of SOC and avoids generalization failures through fine-tuning.
